---
title: "Tenancy & isolation"
description: "How Splendor scopes every request, handle, and record to a single tenant."
---

A tenant is a workspace. Every dataset, source, view, export, and search result belongs to exactly one tenant, and the API never lets data cross that boundary.

## How a tenant is selected

Each request carries the `X-Splendor-Tenant-Id` header. Splendor verifies that your token has access to that tenant before doing anything else. A single token can reach multiple tenants — `GET /v1/me` lists them — but one request only ever acts within the one you name.

```bash
curl https://api.withsplendor.com/v1/search \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $SPLENDOR_TOKEN" \
  -H "X-Splendor-Tenant-Id: acme" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"text": "error", "datasets": ["app-logs"]}'
```

## Isolation guarantees

- **Queries are tenant-scoped.** A search only ever reads datasets in the selected tenant. There is no cross-tenant query.
- **Handles are tenant-bound.** A `search_id`, `view_id`, cursor, or export belongs to the tenant that created it. Presenting a handle under a different tenant fails closed.
- **Records carry their tenant.** Every stored record records its `tenant_id` in [provenance](/concepts/provenance), so isolation holds at the data layer, not just the query layer.

<Warning>
  A handle created in one tenant cannot be read from another, even with a token that can access both. Re-create the search or view under the tenant you intend to use.
</Warning>

## Direct vs platform

There are two ways an organization shows up in Splendor. Both rest on the same tenant boundary above — they differ in *who authenticates* and *who creates tenants*.

**Direct** — your own people use Splendor. Your organization is one tenant; your users sign in (through the [console](https://app.withsplendor.com) or your SSO) and search it directly. A user can belong to several tenants and switch between them — `GET /v1/me` lists them. This is the default: if your team searches your own data, you are here, and there is nothing extra to set up.

**Platform** — you put Splendor underneath your own product, and each of *your* customers has data that must stay isolated. Your backend holds a platform API key and provisions one tenant per customer; your end-users never authenticate to Splendor directly — your application is the only client.

| | Direct | Platform |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Who authenticates | your users, each with their own token | your backend, with one platform API key |
| A tenant is | your organization | one of *your* customers |
| Who creates tenants | Splendor, at onboarding | you, via `POST /v1/platform/tenants` |
| Your end-users | sign in to Splendor | never touch Splendor |

Either way the rule is the same: **a customer is a tenant, never a dataset** — datasets are co-queryable within a tenant and are not an isolation boundary.

If you are building on Splendor, see [Build a platform on Splendor](/guides/platform-patterns) for the model and [Provision tenants for your customers](/guides/provision-tenants) for the API.
